Product mapping is the core of Gyde's recommendation engine. Each multiple-choice or picture-choice option can be linked to one or more products in your Shopify catalogue. When a shopper answers the quiz, Gyde uses their selections to pick and display the most relevant products.

## Opening the Products tab

In the quiz builder, click the **Products** tab (the fourth tab in the top navigation). The panel shows your quiz questions on the left and the mapping interface on the right.

## Mapping products to a choice

1. Click any **multiple-choice** or **picture-choice** question.
2. Each answer option appears in the Products panel.
3. Click **Add products** next to an option and search by product title.
4. Select as many products as you like — Gyde will pick one at random when calculating results.

Repeat this for every option on every question that should contribute to product recommendations. Options with no products mapped are simply ignored during calculation.

## How the results page is built

After a shopper submits the quiz:

1. Gyde collects all the options the shopper selected.
2. For each option that has mapped products, **one product is chosen at random** from that option's list.
3. Duplicates are removed, and the remaining products appear on the results page.

A shopper who selects three choices will see up to three recommended products — one per choice — keeping the results focused rather than overwhelming.

## Mid-quiz product carousel

When a quiz runs in **popup** mode, a product carousel appears immediately after a choice is made. Shoppers can browse the products linked to that option and optionally select one.

- **If the shopper selects a product** from the carousel, only that product is shown on the final results page — regardless of what other choices they make.
- **If the shopper skips** the carousel, normal recommendation logic applies at the end.

This flow is ideal for "I already know what I want — show me options" moments during a quiz.

## Tips for better recommendations

- **Map multiple products per option** — the random selection gives variety across different sessions and prevents the same product always appearing.
- **Think in signals, not filters** — each question choice is a signal about the shopper's preference. Map products that genuinely match that signal, not every product in your catalogue.
- **Combine with conditional logic** — use branching to route shoppers with very specific needs to dedicated choices with tightly curated product lists.

## Viewing quiz responses

To see the raw answers shoppers submitted, go to **Quiz → Submissions**. Each entry shows the full set of answers alongside any product recommendations that were generated. You can filter, export, or delete submissions from this page.
